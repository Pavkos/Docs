Privacy Policy

This policy explains how No Contact: Days Since handles information used for breakup day counting, no-contact tracking, mood check-ins, journals, hard dates, sealed letters, contact logs and paid access.

Last updated: May 7, 2026

Health and Safety

The app is a private tracker and supportive tool. It is not a medical device, does not provide diagnosis or treatment, and is not a substitute for a therapist, physician, crisis line or emergency service.

Information You May Enter

You may choose to enter personal or sensitive information, including:

  • breakup date and last-contact date;
  • mood ratings, emotion tags and short mood notes;
  • no-contact plan details and contact logs;
  • journal entries, drafts, sealed letters and voice-style transcripts;
  • hard dates such as birthdays, anniversaries, holidays, shared places or planned trips;
  • answers to short reflection flows about urges, social media, sleep, setbacks or other breakup-related moments.

Local Storage

The app stores your breakup tracker, no-contact plan, mood entries, journals, hard dates and contact logs locally on your device using iOS app storage. The app does not create a public profile, public journal, social feed or account-based community.

Deleting the app removes local app data from that device. If you export or share any information outside the app in the future, that copy will be controlled by the destination you choose.

Product Analytics

If product analytics are enabled in the app build and you leave analytics sharing turned on, the app may send anonymous product analytics events to Amplitude. Examples include app opened, onboarding completed, flow completed, paywall viewed, purchase started or purchase completed.

Analytics events are limited to event names, feature names, counts, boolean flags and broad buckets such as mood range or breakup day range. The app is designed not to send journal text, sealed letters, message drafts, transcripts, names, notes, hard-date titles, no-contact plan text or other user-written content to analytics.

You can turn anonymous product analytics off in the app's Settings screen.

Voice Transcription

If voice transcription is enabled, audio is sent to a transcription provider such as Deepgram, or to a secure proxy that forwards the request, only when you choose to record and transcribe a voice journal entry. The returned transcript is saved in your private local journal unless you delete it.

If transcription is not configured in a build, voice journal entries remain local and no audio is uploaded for transcription.

StoreKit Purchases

Purchases are handled by Apple through StoreKit. The app may receive product identifiers, entitlement status and transaction status needed to unlock No Contact Plus. We do not receive your full payment card details.

Notifications

If you allow notifications, the app may schedule local reminders for hard dates, preparation flows, day-of support or next-day reflections. Notification content is generated on the device and is not used for advertising.

Tracking, Advertising and Sale of Data

The app does not include advertising SDKs and does not sell personal data. Optional product analytics are used to understand app reliability, feature usage and purchase funnel health. The app does not use your breakup, mood, journal or contact data to track you across apps and websites owned by other companies.

Safety Detection

The app may check text you type into journals or flows for urgent safety language, such as self-harm, suicide, abuse or immediate danger. This is used to show crisis and safety resources. In the current version, this check is performed locally in the app.

Children

The app is not intended for children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the app, please contact support.

Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy as the app changes. If data practices materially change, the policy and App Store privacy information should be updated.
